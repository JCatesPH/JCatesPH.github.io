My Projects

Identification and Quantification of Chemical Vapors with Novel Sensors

May 2019 - Present

Assistant Professor Hauser has been working on impedance-based chemical sensors for a few years, but any chemical sensor requires algorithms to interpret the complex output of the system. The Department of Defense has funded the sensor research to this point, and I have been working independently to write machine learning algorithms in python and tensorflow that will be deployable on our sensor platform. The goal is a robust, cheap, and small system that can identify chemicals of interest.

Efficient Computation of Quantum Properties with a GPU

May 2019 - December 2019

I have been assisting Assistant Professor Tse with implementing algorithms that would calculate quantities like the spin susceptibility of a two-dimensional material. The analytic solutions to these problems can be complicated and ugly expressions. Even the best implementations of Monte-Carlo integration will be very slow calculating these properties when run on the CPU, so we began by trying to use Numba's cuda interface. This was still too slow for values of interest. Current efforts are directed towards a C/C++ cuda version, which builds on gVegas.

Density Functional Theory and Heusler Alloy Stability

January 2019 - May 2019

There has been a lot of interest in Heusler alloys for their tunability and interesting properties. There may, however, be a little too much hype. Theoretical techniques often do not account for defects or phase stability. Assistant Professor Hauser and Graduate Student Sujan Budhathoki wanted to investigate the role of site swapping and alternate phases using Density Functional Theory to guide thin-film experiments. We performed a number of numerical experiments, and the results are being drafted for publication. I am interested in pursuing similar research in the future, especially with interfaces and defects.

Off-Axis Combinatorial Sputtering of Novel Materials

August 2018 - May 2019

Assistant Professor Hauser got his start by synthesizing complex systems with a novel sputtering process. The research is explained further here. I primarily assisted Graduate Student Sujan Budhathoki with material synthesis and record keeping. Recent work has been on producing high quality heterostructures, Heusler alloys, and other metallic films.
