My Projects

Efficient Computation of Quantum Properties with a GPU

May 2019 - Present

I have been assisting Assistant Professor Tse with implementing algorithms that would calculate quantities like the spin susceptibility of a two-dimensional material. The analytic solutions to these problems can be complicated and ugly expressions. Even the best implementations of Monte-Carlo integration will be very slow calculating these properties when run on the CPU, so we began by trying to use Numba's cuda interface. This was still too slow for values of interest. Current efforts are directed towards a C/C++ cuda version, which builds on gVegas.

Identification and Quantification of Chemical Vapors with Novel Sensors

May 2019 - Present

Assistant Professor Hauser has been working on impedance-based chemical sensors for a few years, but any chemical sensor requires algorithms to interpret the complex output of the system. I have been working independently to write machine learning algorithms in python and tensorflow. The goal is a robust, cheap, and small system that can identify chemicals of interest.

Density Functional Theory and Heusler Alloy Stability

January 2019 - May 2019

There has been a lot of interest in Heusler alloys for their tunability and interesting properties. There may, however, be a little too much hype. Theoretical techniques often do not account for defects or phase stability. Assistant Professor Hauser and Graduate Student Sujan Budhathoki wanted to investigate the role of site swapping and alternate phases using Density Functional Theory to guide thin-film experiments.
